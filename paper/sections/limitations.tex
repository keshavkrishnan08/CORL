One limit dominates: the study is simulation-only. We argue the offline-to-online mapping is
the very thing a real-robot user needs before committing to deployment evaluation, and
sim-to-real correlation work~\citep{li2024simpler} supports that conclusions transfer in
direction, but we do not run hardware ourselves. We partly address this by checking whether
our offline metrics predict the published real-robot rankings of generalist policies on an
independent benchmark (Appendix), which validates the cross-policy use of the metrics without
new hardware. Two earlier concerns are resolved by design: we evaluate two architectures
(Diffusion Policy and ACT), so the findings are not specific to a single policy class, and
the (task, seed, architecture) factorisation yields \NRuns{} runs, giving power above 0.95 to
detect a ten-percentage-point gap. We still treat H3, the most stringent test, with care, and
report the full realised power curve. Vision-language-action models remain future work: they
exceed the consumer-GPU budget this study deliberately targets.
